02 / Service design + AR

SeedWalkers

An AR service system using seed cards, public participation and ecological data to reconnect fragmented urban biodiversity.

Type
Service design / environmental design / AR UX
Duration
March to July 2024
Tools
ENVI, ArcGIS, Blender, Unity
Output
Seed-card service system and AR planting flow

Context

Urban greenery can look sufficient while still being ecologically weak because of monoculture and fragmentation.

Challenge

The project needed to connect city-scale carbon and biodiversity issues with everyday public participation.

Process

I used carbon research, biodiversity logic, ENVI/ArcGIS analysis, service blueprinting and AR prototyping to shape the service.

Outcome

The outcome is a seed-card and AR planting service that turns ecological restoration into a participatory journey.

What This Project Demonstrates

What SeedWalkers demonstrates

Service design

Connecting ecological research, public participation and backstage support into a coherent service concept.

Environmental research

Using carbon, biodiversity and vegetation analysis to define why the service matters.

Spatial analysis

Using ENVI and ArcGIS/NDVI evidence to ground the service in urban vegetation conditions.

AR prototyping

Turning seed-card participation into a digital planting flow that people could imagine using in the city.
