01 / Environmental technology

Ear Shield

Ear Shield explores how acoustic monitoring, bionic device design, and an interactive public installation can make the impact of human underwater noise on toothed whales visible.

Type
Speculative product system / installation
Role
Concept, research, prototyping. Confirm team status.
Tools
Python, Arduino, TouchDesigner, Blender
Output
Monitoring concept and exhibition interaction

Context

Toothed whales rely on echolocation for orientation, hunting, and communication. Human-made underwater noise and sonar can interrupt that sensory system, increasing stress and stranding risk.

Challenge

The project needed to make an invisible acoustic problem legible to the public while proposing a monitoring system grounded in whale biology and sound analysis.

Process

The work moved from whale and sound-wave research into bionic suction concepts, Fourier-based acoustic analysis, device system design, and a gesture-based installation using sensor input and whale particle visualization.

Outcome

The PDF shows a public exhibition prototype and stakeholder map. It should be described as an educational speculative prototype unless further real-world validation is confirmed.

Interview & Competency Notes

What Ear Shield demonstrates

Competencies

Problem framing, ecological research, systems thinking, speculative product logic, physical computing, visual prototyping, public communication.

2-minute story

I began with the problem of human underwater noise disrupting toothed whale perception. I researched sperm whale echolocation, translated the issue into a bionic monitoring-device concept, then built an interactive installation so public audiences could experience how noise affects whale navigation.

8-10 minute flow

Start with whale strandings and human noise. Explain why sperm whales rely on sound. Show bionic suction ideation and Python acoustic analysis. Then move into device system design, Arduino/TouchDesigner prototype, exhibition interaction, and stakeholder map. Close by reflecting on scientific validation and ethical constraints for future development.

Need confirmation

Individual or group status, exact duration, exhibition name/date, whether the audio data was real, sampled or simulated, and whether any marine or conservation expert gave feedback.

02 / Service design + AR

SeedWalkers

SeedWalkers turns urban planting into a participatory service experience, using seed cards, AR interaction, and ecological data to reconnect fragmented biodiversity.

Type
Service design / environmental design / AR UX
Duration
March to July 2024
Tools
ENVI, ArcGIS, Blender, Unity
Output
Seed-card service system and AR planting flow

Context

Changsha's urban greening problem is framed through carbon neutrality, vegetation capacity, monoculture, and ecological fragmentation.

Challenge

The project connects city-scale ecology with public participation: how can people help restore biodiversity through a service that is visible, playful, and maintainable?

Process

ENVI, ArcGIS, and NDVI analysis informed the ecological basis. The design then developed into user flows, low-fidelity app screens, stakeholder maps, service blueprints, seed cards, and Unity AR prototypes.

Outcome

The project produced a complete service concept and technical prototype direction. It should be presented as a concept prototype, not a launched civic service.

Portfolio Evidence

Full project archive from the PDF portfolio

These pages show the full service design and environmental research process, from carbon-neutrality framing to biodiversity logic, app flow, stakeholder blueprint, and AR technical implementation.

Interview & Competency Notes

What SeedWalkers demonstrates

Competencies

Service design process, environmental research, spatial analysis, stakeholder mapping, service blueprinting, AR prototyping, translating data into public participation.

2-minute story

SeedWalkers responds to fragmented and homogeneous urban vegetation in Changsha. I connected carbon-neutrality data with biodiversity research, then designed a seed-card and AR planting service that helps residents participate in ecological restoration while government and maintenance actors support the system behind the scenes.

8-10 minute flow

Begin with the climate and urban vegetation context. Explain how biodiversity improves carbon cycling and ecosystem stability. Show ENVI/ArcGIS analysis, then the service framework, user flow, stakeholder map, blueprint, and Unity AR prototype. End by discussing what would need to be tested with city stakeholders and real users.

Need confirmation

Individual or team status, whether seed species were validated by experts, whether any user testing happened, and whether the carbon figures should be rechecked before publication.

03 / AI + critical interaction

Machine Mapping

Machine Mapping uses a machine-learning metaphor, participatory discussion, and data visualization to question how color stereotypes are formed and repeated.

Type
Interactive experiment / critical design
Role
Research, interaction, creative technology. Confirm.
Tools
Arduino, Python, Vosk, Blender
Output
Machine-brain visualization prototype

Context

Color meanings can feel natural, but they are shaped by culture, markets, personal experience, and social agreement.

Challenge

Instead of judging participants, the project reflects their own associations back through a machine system, asking who gives color its meaning.

Process

Participants discuss colors, the system extracts keywords through speech recognition, searches related images, processes color blocks, and visualizes the machine's changing memory in Blender.

Outcome

The result is a reflective prototype and documented video that makes social cognition and machine bias easier to discuss.

Full project archive from the PDF portfolio

Interview & Competency Notes

What Machine Mapping demonstrates

Competencies

Critical AI thinking, participatory interaction design, technical experimentation, data visualization, cultural research, handling ambiguity, reflective design.

2-minute story

Machine Mapping explores how color stereotypes are socially learned and reproduced by machines. I used group discussion, speech recognition, image search and Blender visualization to show how human language and cultural assumptions can become machine memory.

8-10 minute flow

Start with the question of who gives color meaning. Explain research into cultural, commercial and social construction of color. Show the machine-learning metaphor, participant discussion stages, Python/Vosk keyword extraction, image collection, color synthesis and Blender brain-space visualization. Close with what the prototype reveals about bias entering a system through social input.

Need confirmation

Project duration, individual or group status, whether all coding was done by you, whether it was publicly exhibited, and whether participant feedback was collected.

04 / Social design

Cross-Culture Icebreaker

A group project designing a social pack and board-game experience to help Chinese international students exchange cultural backgrounds and build early connections.

Type
Service design / social design / game experience
Role
Group project. Personal role to confirm.
Methods
Survey, personas, 5W1H, system map, prototype
Output
Social package and board-game kit

Context

Chinese international students may face social anxiety, cultural conflict, language barriers, and a lack of low-pressure opportunities to connect.

Challenge

The design needed to break the ice naturally, creating structure before spontaneity so students could interact without feeling forced into direct self-presentation.

Process

The team used a 117-person questionnaire, personas, 5W1H framing, service system mapping, social-pack flows, and game mechanics to create a two-part interaction system.

Outcome

The PDF documents the physical prototype, demo process, business canvas, and participant quotes. Long-term social impact still needs validation.

What Cross-Culture Icebreaker demonstrates

Competencies

User research, questionnaire synthesis, persona development, social design, service system mapping, game mechanics, prototyping, group collaboration.

2-minute story

This group project responds to social anxiety and cultural adaptation challenges faced by Chinese international students. We used survey data, personas and service mapping to design a social pack and board-game experience that creates structured, low-pressure opportunities for cross-cultural exchange.
