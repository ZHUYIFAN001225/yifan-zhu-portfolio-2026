Future Experience Prototype

Biodiversity Clock

A future-facing experience prototype exploring ecological awareness and multi-species coexistence in Pollok Country Park.

Hero image / render / project video
Context
GSA project, Glasgow, UK
Role
Concept logic, research synthesis, technical prototyping
Methods
Field research, stakeholder mapping, Three Horizons, HMW framing
Tools
Figma, Miro, Blender, AI-assisted workflow, visual storytelling
01

The Challenge

Public ecological issues are often difficult to experience directly. The project needed to translate biodiversity, visitor behaviour and multi-species relationships into a clear experience concept that could be discussed by designers, park staff and public audiences.

02

Research & Systems Framing

Field research in Pollok Country Park and conversations with park staff grounded the project in a real visitor and ecological context. Stakeholder mapping and Three Horizons were used to understand how current behaviours could shift toward future coexistence.

Stakeholder map
Three Horizons / journey map
03

Concept & Prototype

The concept turns ecological relationships into a future experience prototype: a designed moment where visitors can reflect on biodiversity over time rather than seeing nature as a static background.

Prototype sequence / scenario visuals
04

Outcome & Reflection

The project demonstrates how systems thinking and visual prototyping can make public ecological futures tangible. It also shows a working method that connects research, narrative, spatial imagination and technical making.

Key

What this project should prove

  • Ability to translate complex systems into discussable concepts.
  • Strength in visual prototyping and future experience storytelling.
  • Service-design thinking beyond interface screens.
  • Comfort working between research, strategy and making.
