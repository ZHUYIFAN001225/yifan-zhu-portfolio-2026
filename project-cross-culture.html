OverviewContextResearchDevelopmentOutcomeImpactReflection

Context

Chinese international students may face social anxiety, language barriers and limited low-pressure opportunities to connect.

Challenge

The project needed to break the ice naturally without forcing uncomfortable direct interaction.

Process

The team used survey data, personas, service mapping, social-pack flows and game mechanics.

Outcome

The outcome is a two-part social system: a rotating social pack and a collaborative board-game kit.

What This Project Demonstrates

What Cross-Culture Icebreaker demonstrates

User research

Using interviews, questionnaire data and personas to define barriers to early social connection.

Social design

Creating low-pressure interaction formats that help students exchange culture without forced self-presentation.

Service mapping

Connecting students, organisers, schools, communities and sponsors around a repeatable social experience.

Game mechanics

Using physical prompts, routes, puzzle zones and cultural questions to structure meaningful interaction.
